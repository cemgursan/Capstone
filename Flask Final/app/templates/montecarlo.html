Monte Carlo Simulation

  Monte Carlo simulations are a broad class of computational algorithms that rely on repeated random sampling to obtain numerical results.
  The underlying concept is to use randomness to solve problems that might be deterministic in principle. The main idea behind this method is that the results are computed based on repeated random sampling and statistical analysis.
  At every Monte Carlo run, the stock price moves randomly according to its previous mean and standard deviation. This gives a deterministic range for where the stock price can be in the future.
  Different colored lines represent different possibilities where the stock price can reach.
 
  Please choose a Stock to simulate in the drop-down box below.
  Please write the amount of simulation days in the second textbox that you would like the simulation to continue
  Please write the amount of simulation runs in the third textbox that you would like to experiment with.
  Please write the confidence interval for the Histogram Distribution for various analysis in the third textbox
